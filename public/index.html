How to Map Your Evacuation Route

Watch this tutorial to learn how to use the mapping tool

  1. Enter your starting location - where you began your evacuation (you can type an address or click on the map)
  2. Enter your ending location - your final destination (you can type an address or click on the map)
  3. Add stops - click "Add Stop" to search for places by name, or click directly on the map to add intermediate stops
  4. Adjust your route - drag the blue route line to match the actual roads you traveled
  5. Use "Revert" to undo changes while keeping your start/end points
  6. Submit when ready - you can submit multiple routes for different evacuation events

Complex Routes with Return Trips

Example: Home → School → Destination → Back Home → Back to Destination

Map this as ONE continuous route with all stops in order. The numbered sequence (1, 2, 3...) shows your actual path, including any return trips.

Submit separate routes only for different evacuation events (different days, times, or completely separate trips).

Map Legend:

A = Starting Location

B = Ending Location

1,2,3... = Stops (in order)

Privacy Protection

Your privacy is protected: We apply random buffer zones (100-200m) around all locations in your route to ensure your exact locations remain private while allowing us to analyze evacuation patterns. The specific buffer size is not recorded.
